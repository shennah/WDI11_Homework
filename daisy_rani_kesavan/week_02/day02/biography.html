Biography of Grace Hopper

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper, was an American computer scientist and United States Navy Rear Admiral.

Nickname(s) "Amazing Grace" Born: December 9, 1906 Died: January 1, 1992, Spouse: Vincent Foster Hopper (m. 1930–1945 Education: Yale University (1934), Yale University (1930), Vassar College (1924–1928), Wardlaw-Hartridge School She was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer in 1944,[2] invented the first compiler for a computer programming language,[3][4][5][6][7] and was one of those who popularized the idea of machine-independent programming languages which led to the development of COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages.
